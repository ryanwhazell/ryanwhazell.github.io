Rolling for a Magical Item

Step 1: Roll to determine what type of item it is (1d8)

Before anything, we must determine the type of item. Roll a d8 and refer to the list below:

  • If you rolled a 1: You found an item with a special property. Reroll until you get a 2-6 to determine what type.
  • If you rolled a 2: you got a Ring
  • If you rolled a 3: you got a Staff
  • If you rolled a 4: you got a Weapon
  • If you rolled a 5: you got Armor
  • If you rolled a 6: you got a Wondrous Item
  • If you rolled a 7: you found an Artifact. Skip steps 2-4 and proceed to "Artifacts"
  • If you rolled an 8: You found an item with a special property. Reroll until you get a 2-6 to determine what type.

Note: If you rolled both an 8 and a 1, then your item has both of the special properties. Let the dungeon master know (only they will know what to do with this information), and re-roll until you get between 2 and 6

Step 2: Determine Rarity (1d3)

Roll a d3, or a d4 until you get a result between 1-3. This will determine if your item is a Minor, Medium, or Major magical item.

Step 3: Determine your final item (1d100)

Once you know the item type, and the rarity, the DM will know what table he needs you to roll on. Roll a d100 to determine the effect or power of your item. If you get 100 on your roll, then your item has two effects. Roll two more times to determine these effects. An item will not have more than two effects by default, so ignore any further 100's.

Step 4: Give your results to the Dungeon Master

Depending on what you rolled you probably have the following numbers to pass off to the dungeon master so he can find what item you recieved. You will probably still need to get it identified to know what it does for sure.

Make sure you record all of your rolls' results. You should (probably) have:

  • An item type, possibly with one or both special qualities (meaning in step one you rolled 1, 8, or both)
  • A rarity
  • One or two numbers between 1 and 99 to determine effect(s)

Once you have this information, record it, save it, and give it to the dungeon master and they will let you know what item you have found.

Artifacts

If you rolled a 7 in step 1, then you should ignore Steps 2-4. Instead, flip a coin. If you got tails, you discovered a minor artifact. If you got heads, you discovered a major artifact.

Minor Artifact: There are 18 possible minor artifacts. Roll a d20 until you get a result between 1 and 18

Major Artifact: There are 15 possible major artifacts. Roll a d20 until you get a result between 1 and 15

Once you've determined your number for a major or minor artifact, inform the DM so he can produce the item you found.
